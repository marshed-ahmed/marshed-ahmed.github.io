## About Me

**Arafath Ahmed Marshed** is a recent graduate of the Master of Science program in Physics at Shahjalal University of Science and Technology (SUST), Bangladesh. His academic training and research experience are centered on experimental optics, with a particular emphasis on nonlinear light–matter interactions, precision optical measurements, and photonic techniques for materials characterization.

## Academic Background

Marshed earned both his B.Sc. (Honours) and M.Sc. degrees in Physics from Shahjalal University of Science and Technology, graduating with distinction. His undergraduate and graduate studies provided a rigorous foundation in core areas of modern physics, including nonlinear optics, laser physics, solid-state physics, and quantum computation. This coursework has supported his development as an experimental researcher with strong theoretical grounding.

## Research Interests

- Nonlinear optics and ultrafast laser spectroscopy  
- Light–matter interactions in molecular and condensed-phase systems  
- Optical characterization of pharmaceutical and biomaterials  
- Quantum optics and quantum sensing  
- Structured light and precision optical metrology  

## Research & Scholarly Activities

Marshed has presented his research at national and international scientific meetings, including the *International Conference on Advances in Physics (ICAP‑2025)* and the *National Conference on Physics 2025*. His work includes the application of Z‑scan spectroscopy to pharmaceutical quality assessment as well as precision studies of the Goos–Hänchen shift using custom-built optical setups and quantitative image analysis techniques.

His experimental research integrates table‑top laser systems, optical alignment, and high‑precision data acquisition. Complementing this work, he employs computational tools for modeling nonlinear optical phenomena and for processing and analyzing experimental data.

## Skills & Tools

**Experimental:** Laser alignment and stabilization, nonlinear optical characterization (Z‑scan), spectroscopy, beam profiling, precision optical measurements, and optomechanical system design (including 3D‑printed components).

**Computational:** Python (NumPy, SciPy, Matplotlib, OpenCV, scikit‑learn), data analysis, signal processing, image analysis, and numerical modeling. Familiarity with Mathematica and COMSOL Multiphysics.

**Research Practices:** Experimental design, instrumentation, data acquisition, uncertainty analysis, and scientific writing.

## Academic Goals

Marshed is seeking doctoral training in experimental optics and photonics, with the aim of contributing to research in areas such as quantum sensing, ultrafast laser technologies, and biophotonics. His long‑term goal is to pursue an academic research career that bridges fundamental investigations of light–matter interactions with practical and impactful applications. He is also motivated to contribute to inclusive teaching and mentorship, particularly in contexts where access to advanced research infrastructure is limited.
